How to Apply for a DTF Transfer

Follow these simple steps to get started with a DTF transfer.

1

Prepare Your Fabric

👕

Ensure the fabric is clean, dry, and wrinkle-free for the best adhesion.

2

Position the Transfer

🎯

Place the DTF transfer on the fabric with the printed side facing down.

3

Press with Heat

🔥
  • Set your heat press to 300-325°F.
  • Apply firm pressure for 10-15 seconds.
4

Cool & Peel

❄️
  • Let it cool fully before peeling.
  • Peel slowly and evenly for a smooth finish.
5

Final Press (Optional)

✨

For extra durability, cover with a Teflon sheet and press again for 5 seconds.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about DTF printing

What is DTF printing, and how does it differ from other printing methods?

Direct-to-Film (DTF) printing is a technique where designs are printed onto a special film and then transferred to various fabrics using heat and pressure. This method allows for vibrant, detailed prints on materials like cotton, polyester, and blends, offering versatility and durability compared to traditional printing methods.

Can I provide my own design for printing, and what file formats do you accept?

Yes, you can submit your custom designs for printing. Accepted file formats include PNG, PDF, and TIFF. For optimal results, ensure your artwork is high-resolution, preferably 300 DPI or higher.

How quickly can I receive my DTF order?

We offer same-day printing services for urgent orders. Depending on your location and chosen shipping method, you can receive your order within 24 hours.

Do you offer bulk or wholesale pricing for larger orders?

Yes, we provide competitive pricing for bulk and wholesale orders. For detailed information on pricing and discounts, please contact our sales team at info@dtfmiamiprints.com.

Are DTF transfers suitable for all fabric types?

DTF transfers are versatile and can be applied to various fabrics, including cotton, polyester, nylon, elastane, textile blends, linen, soft shell, felt, and even genuine leather. However, it's essential to follow the specific ironing instructions for each material to ensure optimal results.
